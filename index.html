Professional Personal

Hailing from Portland, Oregon, I am a dedicated software developer.

My dedication has taken me from Lewis & Clark College for a B.A. in Computer Science & Mathematics, to University of Oregon, where I am earning my Masters in Computer Sciece, with an emphasis on Artificial Intelligence.twitter

As a student, I spent 6 months working with Professor Daniel Lowd on researching spam occurences on Twitter. We examined the social web and its relation to the probability of a user being a spammer. I have spent the last year acting as a teaching assistant for 100 level classes, resulting in me leading multiple labs of 20+ students each quarter.

As a professional developer, I have created and supported business class software, managed databases, maintained legacy programs and websites, and worked on Android applications.

My most recent project was FlareMail, an Android application I did as an employee of Uncorked Studios. I was the creator and sole Android developer on the project. A gallery of FlareMail screenshots can be seen here. It is available on Google Play.

Before that, I worked on a couple large projects for Uncorked Studios. One was the Safecast website. Safecast is an international, non-profit project created as a result of the earthquake that hit Japan in 2011. Safecast collects and provides up-to-date information about radiation levels in Japan, collected by independent individuals and organizations.

The other large project was an experimental mobile 3D mapping project for a very well-respected ad agency. Sadly, this project never saw the light of day, but did provide me with a learning experience about seeing a project get shelved, working with a group of many developers across multiple platforms, and dealing with rapid prototyping and ever-shifting design concepts and designs, all while working on a platform I had no prior experience on. As a result, I came out the other side a more well-rounded developer.

In my spare time, I am studying OpenGL ES on the Android system. Although I do spend a large amount of time on Android, my professional interests are pretty varied. I enjoy creating projects, whether for desktop applications or mobile, for the web or otherwise.
